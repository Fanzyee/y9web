Cholera

1.4~4 MILLION CASES

Cholera is a disease that is transmitted through food and water that have been contaminated with feces from a person or organism affected by Cholera. Cholera is most likely to be found and spread in places with inadequate water treatment, poor sanitation, and inadequate hygiene. This makes the disease substantially deadly and infectious.

143000 DEATHS

If left untreated, cholera can be fatal in a matter of hours, even in previously healthy people. However, efforts put out by the WHO, governmental organizations, and NGOs are, step by step, putting an end to cholera in these less fortunate countries.
